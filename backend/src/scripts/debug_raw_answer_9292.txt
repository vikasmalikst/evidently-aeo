The best compact EVs with fast charging capability are the Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Kia EV6
, which use an advanced 800-volt architecture to achieve some of the fastest charging speeds available on the market. Other strong options include certain Tesla models and the Volkswagen ID.3 Pro S. Top Compact EVs with Fast Charging Hyundai Ioniq 5
Reviewers praise its peak charging rate of up to 235 kW
, enabling a 10% to 80% charge in as little as 18 minutes under optimal conditions.
It features a spacious, retro-inspired design and is built on an efficient 800-volt platform.
Kia EV6
Sharing the same 800-volt architecture as the Ioniq 5, the EV6 offers similar impressive charging speeds, also reaching 10% to 80% charge in about 18 minutes
.
It is described as a sleek, sporty crossover that is fun to drive and has a roomy interior.
Volkswagen ID.3 Pro S
This compact EV boasts an impressive range (up to 568 km WLTP) and supports up to 185 kW DC fast charging.
According to Electra, its high efficiency and accessible price make it a strong contender for high-mileage drivers.
Tesla Model 3 / Model Y
Tesla models benefit from access to the extensive and reliable Supercharger network.
They can reach a peak charging power of 250 kW
, allowing the Model Y to charge from 10% to 80% in about 15 minutes at a Tesla Supercharger.
Reviewers note the excellent overall charging experience due to the network's reliability and number of chargers per station. Key Factors for Fast Charging When choosing a fast-charging EV, consider these factors: 800-Volt Architecture
: Vehicles with an 800-volt system, such as the Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Kia EV6, can generally accept higher power inputs and maintain faster charging speeds for longer periods than traditional 400-volt systems.
Charging Curve
: The average charging power is often more important than the peak power. Some EVs reach a high peak only for a short time before significantly slowing down, while others maintain a more consistent, albeit lower, high speed.
Network Infrastructure
: Access to a robust, reliable, and high-powered charging network, like the Tesla Supercharger network (now accessible to many other brands via the NACS port/adapter), is critical for practical, real-world fast charging on road trips
.
